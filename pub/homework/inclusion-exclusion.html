Inclusion-Exclusion Principle

Problems marked with an asterisk (*) are considered to be challenge problems. You should still try them, but we will discuss them in class.

Problem 1: Multiples of 222 or 333

How many whole numbers from 111 to 100100100 are multiples of 222 or 333?

Solution

There are 505050 multiples of 222 and 333333 multiples of 333. But we have double-counted the multiples of 666, of which there are 161616. So there are 50+33−16=6750 + 33 - 16 = 6750+33−16=67 whole numbers from 111 to 100100100 which are multiples of 222 or 333.

Problem 2: Linguistic Conundrum

In my class of 100100100 people, 777 speak Yoruba, 555 speak Wolof, and 222 speak both Yoruba and Wolof. How many people speak at least one of Yoruba or Wolof?

Solution

By the inclusion-exclusion principle, 7+5=2+x7 + 5 = 2 + x7+5=2+x, where xxx is the number of people who speak Yoruba or Wolof. Thus x=10x = 10x=10 people.

Problem 3: Farmville

I have 10000 m210000\,\mathrm{m}^210000m2 of land, all of which is suitable for growing either potatoes or tomatoes, or both. 8000 m28000\,\mathrm{m}^28000m2 is suitable for growing potatoes. 3000 m23000\,\mathrm{m}^23000m2 is suitable for growing tomatoes. How much land is suitable for growing both potatoes and tomatoes?

Solution

By the inclusion-exclusion principle, 10000 m2+x=8000 m2+3000 m210000\,\mathrm{m}^2 + x = 8000\,\mathrm{m}^2 + 3000\,\mathrm{m}^210000m2+x=8000m2+3000m2, where xxx represents the area of land suitable for growing both. Thus x=1000 m2x = 1000\,\mathrm{m}^2x=1000m2.

Problem 4: Nonnegative Solutions to an Equation*

How many non-negative integer solutions mmm, nnn, ppp are there to m+n+p≤10m + n + p \le 10m+n+p≤10 subject to m≤3m \le 3m≤3, n≤4n \le 4n≤4, and p≤5p \le 5p≤5?

Solution

There are 4×5×6=1204 \times 5 \times 6 = 1204×5×6=120 total triplets of mmm, nnn, ppp within the specified intervals. We will reduce this number by the combinations whose sum exceed 101010. Those are actually fairly rare: the maximum possible sum is 3+4+5=123 + 4 + 5 = 123+4+5=12, for which there is only one way of obtaining this sum. The other invalid sum is 111111, and this can be achieved by reducing one of mmm, nnn, and ppp by 111, so there are three other invalid sums. By deducting the 444 invalid sums, we find there are 116116116 eligible solutions to this equation.
